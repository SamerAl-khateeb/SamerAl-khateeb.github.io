About me

I am an assistant professor at the Department of Journalism, Media, and Computing, College of Arts and Sciences, at Creighton University. I study Deviance in Social Media, e.g., the Online Deviant Groups (ODGs) who use the Internet, specifically social media channels, to recruit individuals who are interested in conducting deviant acts that in many cases pose a security threat to the society. My dissertation title "Studying Online Deviant Groups (ODGs): A Socio-Technical Approach Leveraging Social Network Analysis (SNA) and Social Cyber Forensics (SCF) Techniques". The three main components of my dissertation were: studying the phenomenon of Deviant Cyber Flash Mobs (DCFMs), the use of Bots by deviant groups (e.g., ISIL and anti-NATO Propagandist), and the use of Social Cyber Forensics (SCF) to uncover or infer different relations within or among different deviant groups. I am enthusiastic to learn new things that are applicable to real-world problems.
